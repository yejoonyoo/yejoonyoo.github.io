Embodying Social AI Agents for Simulating Conflict to Teach Conflict Resolution Strategies

This ongoing research project in collaboration with Stanford University delves into the potential of embodied AI agents in simulating conflict situations to teach conflict resolution strategies. Through embodied social AI, we aim to explore the effects of virtual interactions on emotional regulation and strategic decision-making.

Background

Our research draws on principles from human-computer interaction, AI, and conflict psychology. We are particularly interested in how embodying virtual agents can impact learning outcomes in emotionally charged scenarios. The project involves designing interactive simulations where users engage with AI agents that model realistic responses to conflict, enabling practice in handling real-life situations.

Methodology

The research employs a mix of quantitative and qualitative methodologies, including surveys, structured interviews, and behavioral analytics from the simulations. Participantsâ€™ responses and strategies are analyzed to assess the efficacy of the AI-driven interactions in improving emotional regulation and conflict resolution skills.

Future Directions

We envision this research as a stepping stone toward more personalized educational tools in conflict resolution training. Future studies will explore adaptive AI behaviors based on users' interaction styles, creating a more tailored learning experience. The findings could inform the development of educational AI across various domains.
